Mercedes-Benz 300 SLR Uhlenhaut Coupe (1955)

The Mercedes-Benz 300 SLR Uhlenhaut Coupe is a rare and iconic vehicle, with only two units ever produced.

Specifications

  • Engine: Type M 196 S, Twin Spark Inline-8 with dry sump lubrication
  • Horsepower: 277.53 hp
  • Top Speed: 180 mph (290 km/h)
  • Transmission: 5-speed manual

History

In 2022, one of the two 300 SLR Uhlenhaut Coupes was sold at a private auction for a record-breaking €135 million (approximately $142 million), making it the most expensive car ever sold. This sale surpassed the previous record held by a 1962 Ferrari 250 GTO.

Features

  • Only two units produced
  • Fixed-roof coupe version of the 300 SLR
  • Designed for racing, but never competed due to Mercedes-Benz’s withdrawal from motorsports
  • Features advanced design and engineering for its time
